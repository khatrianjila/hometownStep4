Capital City of Nepal

Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal, is a vibrant city nestled in a valley surrounded by the Himalayan mountains. Known for its rich history and cultural heritage, it is home to numerous UNESCO World Heritage Sites, including the ancient Durbar Square and the sacred Pashupatinath Temple. The city's streets are filled with bustling markets, traditional architecture, and a mix of modern and ancient influences. Kathmandu is a hub for trekkers and adventurers, often serving as a gateway to the world-famous Everest Base Camp. Despite the challenges of urbanization and the aftermath of the 2015 earthquake, it remains a city full of life and resilience.
